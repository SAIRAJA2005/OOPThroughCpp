Conventional programming (also called procedural programming)

Disadvantages:
1. Lack of Reusability
2. Poor Scalability
3. Difficulty in Debugging and Maintenance
4. Harder to Model Real-World Problems
5. Less Secure Data Handling
6. Not Suitable for Very Large Systems
7. Limited Abstraction
8. Sequential Execution
9. Global State Issues
10. Less Flexibility in Code Organization
11. Doesnt support Encapsulation, Abstraction, and Inheritance
